WHO IT’S FOR

Founders and operators who want clearer pathways, not more content.

Best fit: founders and operators of 1–50 person companies—service businesses, boutiques, agencies, clinics, and product teams—who are already in motion and want clearer operating patterns.

It is for people who are willing to write things down, assign ownership, and follow a sequence. It pairs well with Texas Movement Consulting when stakes and complexity are high.

It is a poor fit for founders who want purely motivational material, generic “10 tips” content, or unstructured brainstorming sessions.

Founders who use FounderLink tend to share a pattern.

They care about systems, not just growth hacks. They are willing to slow down to sequence decisions, write records, and build workflows that survive stress.

FounderLink exists as a support lane: it gives you patterns and tools; Consulting helps you design and implement systems when the work needs deeper, bespoke attention.

OPERATING PATHWAYS

Clear, documented pathways for common founder decisions.

FounderLink pathways are designed to answer “what do I do next?” with structure, not vibes. Each pathway gives you steps, owners, and checkpoints, and points to when you may need deeper consulting work.

Pathway
Cash and runway decisions.

A structured way to think about cash, expenses, and runway—what to cut, what to protect, and how to make the decision legible to your team and stakeholders.

Pathway
Hiring and role design.

Steps for defining roles, responsibilities, and expectations so new hires plug into systems instead of adding more chaos.

Pathway
Product and service focus.

A way to decide what to stop, start, and keep in your product or service mix so effort concentrates on what actually moves the company.

Pathway
Systems upgrades.

A sequence for upgrading systems—operations, AI workflows, and documentation—without breaking what already works.

PLAYBOOKS & TOOLS

Lightweight tools that fit into real founder work.

FounderLink tools are meant to be used, not admired. They are simple enough to run inside your existing stack—docs, spreadsheets, project tools—and strong enough to support better decisions.

Tool
Weekly operating review template.

A repeatable format for founders to review operations, cash, and key decisions each week and produce a short record that the team can see.

Tool
Decision log schema.

A schema for logging decisions—what was decided, why, and what needs to be revisited—so your future self is not guessing.

Tool
Owner map.

A simple way to map who owns what in your company so work stops falling between people and projects.

Tool
Systems change checklist.

A checklist for making changes to systems without blowing up operations—testing, communication, rollouts, and fallback plans.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Is FounderLink a coaching program?

No. FounderLink is a lane for playbooks and operating pathways. Coaching and deeper system design live in Texas Movement Consulting.

Who is FounderLink for?

Founders and operators who are already in motion and want clearer operating patterns. Idea-stage founders with no motion yet may need different support first.

How is this different from generic founder content?

It is systems-first, practical, and built to sit inside an operating system. It avoids hype and generic advice in favor of structured pathways and tools.

Does FounderLink replace consulting?

No. FounderLink gives you patterns and tools. When stakes, complexity, or risk are high, Texas Movement Consulting is the place for scoped, bespoke systems work.

How does FounderLink connect to the rest of Texas Movement?

It shares the same systems lens and connects directly to Consulting, Performance, Media, Distribution, Health, HERO, and Reparations as part of the broader ecosystem.
